# What is Open Finance?

Now that we have level set on the differences of closed vs. open systems, let‚Äôs dig a bit deeper from a capital markets perspective, specifically on the ‚Äúdecentralized‚Äù bucket.

The digital asset space moves fast with new projects & technologies frequently released (a la the beauty of  [open source cryptonetwork effects](https://a16z.com/2019/03/02/cooperatives-cryptonetworks/)). A lot of phrases get thrown around in different context ‚Äî all of which mean different things to many of the ecosystem participants.

One of these areas is the decentralized finance vs. open finance arena. What do these 2 terms mean and why should you care?

The promise of the internet was to unlock information / data creation exponentially ([which it has done](http://mirror.dlib.org/dlib/may09/mestl/05mestl.html)), but one key bit of infrastructure that was left out was inherent value transfer at the lowest protocol level (see  [Joel Monegro‚Äôs Fat Protocols](https://www.usv.com/writing/2016/08/fat-protocols/) thesis, as an example of this type of thinking).

So, with information out running in the wild today ([technologically speaking](https://www.mckinsey.com/~/media/mckinsey/business%20functions/mckinsey%20digital/our%20insights/twenty-five%20years%20of%20digitization%20ten%20insights%20into%20how%20to%20play%20it%20right/mgi-briefing-note-twenty-five-years-of-digitization-may-2019.ashx)), why can‚Äôt our capital flow freely without barriers¬≤ too? This is where the promise of digital assets and decentralized networks, i.e. where no single entity has exclusive control over data (information, value, or otherwise) flow, come into play.

Within these networks, there are generally two types of approaches to innovation within the ‚Äòfinance vertical‚Äô, defined¬≥ as:

-   **Decentralized Finance**: Networks that run pseudo-anonymous, could potentially be censorship resistant, cater to a global market, and are considerably more transparent thanks to operations running on public permissionless blockchains.
-   **Open Finance**: Open finance points towards an unbundling of banking services where API-enabled startups provide niche services in a limited geographical region. They require AML/KYC and are more often B2C focused. There are many different types of companies competing in the open finance fintech vertical, with  [many falling in the ‚Äòclosed capital‚Äô bucket](https://interactives.cbinsights.com/us-banks-fintech-investments/).

So, why do these two definitions matter? It comes down to whether or not one believes  [a world after capital](http://worldaftercapital.org/)  is possible. Where value moves freely without borders. Access is democratized. And the  [scarcity](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/scarcity.asp)  of capital is no longer an impediment to growth. Decentralized finance, in essence.

Yes, what we are referring to here is certainly idealistic and potentially a long way out from mainstream, but this thinking is core to our value structure and company ethos here at  [Alkemi](https://alkemi.network/). It is core to the ethos of the crypto community globally and a mission that we are focused on executing on, incorporating this spirit into each and every business decision we make as an organization.

To get there, opening up existing capital markets is vital. In order for the natively digital asset class to be truly impactful, institutions (i.e. gate keepers of the traditional economy) need to recognize and embrace the value of participating in the 24/7 global, digital economy, otherwise, in a  [Moore‚Äôs Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moore%27s_law)  world, technology will no doubt leave these firms behind.

This is where open & decentralized finance come into play and why we at Alkemi are attacking this space head on with our open prime solution.

To bridge the gap between the old and the new.

# Why do we Care About Institutional Capital?

Before we get to what we are building, let‚Äôs level set once more on why institutional capital matters for the space.

One of the big questions that remains unanswered in the programmable capital era is who should have a say where this value accumulates? Mr. Market? Institutions? Governments? Humanity?

In today‚Äôs mainstream traditional capital markets, governments, banks, and high flying asset allocators  [control capital flows](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/c/capital_conrol.asp). These gatekeepers of the old guard govern and restrict capital in an analog format still operating as if we live in a pre-internet, disconnected world (see context  [here](https://www.ft.com/content/64e22402-3e38-11e8-bcc8-cebcb81f1f90)  and  [here](https://www.weforum.org/agenda/2018/06/law-too-slow-for-new-tech-how-keep-up/)).

When these institutions see reason to come into the digital finance arena, it is only then that this category will become ‚Äúmainstream,‚Äù facilitating a more level playing field than finance 1.0 with real capital markets use cases (e.g. speculative investing, banking services, payments, structuring, debt financing etc.) opened up for all to access.

>>> In short, the gatekeepers of old need to embrace the tides of change. The tides of change that raise all boats. Only then, will this new financial system become mainstream and access to greater value than ever before will be unlocked.
